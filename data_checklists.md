# Data Checklists

Use these checklists after identifying the likely value path. Compare recommended data with the user's selected available data.

## 1. Revenue Growth

Required data: impacted traffic/leads/customers, conversion or order baseline, conversion/order lift, average order value, gross margin, attribution assumption.  
Optional data: control group, campaign calendar, seasonality, channel spend, repeat purchase, customer segment.  
Suggested owner: sales owner, ecommerce owner, marketing owner, finance partner.  
Why it matters: revenue value depends on incremental business impact, not total sales.  
Calculation relevance: supports incremental gross profit.  
Common pitfalls: attributing all sales growth to the product; ignoring promotions, price, channel spend, or seasonality.

## 2. Cost Saving

Required data: original annual cost, new annual cost, cancelled or reduced vendor/license/outsourcing/material cost, saving start date.  
Optional data: contract terms, budget line, procurement record, one-time transition cost.  
Suggested owner: finance, procurement, product owner, vendor owner.  
Why it matters: direct saving should be budget-visible or contract-visible.  
Calculation relevance: supports annual direct saving.  
Common pitfalls: calling theoretical labor efficiency a saving when no budget or external spend changed.

## 3. Cost Avoidance

Required data: counterfactual baseline, avoided FTE/external days/procurement/duplicate build, unit cost, attribution ratio.  
Optional data: demand pipeline, capacity forecast, historical project cost, staffing plan.  
Suggested owner: business owner, finance, PMO, platform owner.  
Why it matters: avoided cost depends on a credible "without product" scenario.  
Calculation relevance: supports avoided FTE, avoided external service, or avoided duplicate build estimates.  
Common pitfalls: treating avoided future spend as confirmed saving; weak baseline scenario.

## 4. Productivity Gain

Required data: active users, usage frequency, labor time saved per use, annual cycles or transaction volume, role cost, discount factor.  
Optional data: user survey, time-and-motion sample, before/after process map, adoption trend.  
Suggested owner: product owner, business process owner, HR/finance for role cost.  
Why it matters: productivity value depends on real labor time saved, not elapsed time.  
Calculation relevance: supports labor-hour value.  
Common pitfalls: counting waiting-time reduction as labor saving; no discount for adoption or evidence uncertainty.

## 5. Experience / Service Quality Improvement

Required data: target users, current experience baseline, NPS/CSAT/complaints/waiting time/completion rate, adoption or repeat usage.  
Optional data: user feedback themes, support tickets, retention, churn, service recovery cost.  
Suggested owner: CX owner, service owner, product owner, support owner.  
Why it matters: experience improvement is often a leading indicator before monetization.  
Calculation relevance: supports proxy metrics and later linkage to revenue/productivity/cost.  
Common pitfalls: monetizing satisfaction directly without causal evidence.

## 6. Decision Quality / Management Visibility

Required data: decision users, decision scenario, decision frequency, manual reporting time saved, action taken from insight, business metric affected.  
Optional data: alert lead time, error reduction, meeting reduction, inventory/expense/sales improvement.  
Suggested owner: business owner, BI/data owner, finance if value is monetized.  
Why it matters: dashboard value depends on changed decisions or reduced manual work.  
Calculation relevance: supports reporting productivity and attributed business improvement.  
Common pitfalls: using page views as value evidence; no evidence of decision change.

## 7. Deadline-Driven Business Value

Required data: deadline, reason for deadline, affected business amount or opportunity, delay period, decay or missed-window assumption.  
Optional data: campaign plan, launch plan, customer commitment, regulatory date.  
Suggested owner: business owner, campaign owner, launch owner, legal/compliance if regulatory.  
Why it matters: time value exists only when delay changes economic or risk outcomes.  
Calculation relevance: supports delay loss or missed-window value.  
Common pitfalls: treating internal urgency as economic time criticality.

## 8. Operational Continuity

Required data: affected operation, order/production/service volume, downtime or failure frequency, loss per hour/day, SLA, workaround cost.  
Optional data: incident history, recovery time, dependency map, criticality rating.  
Suggested owner: operations owner, supply chain owner, IT owner, service owner.  
Why it matters: low visible usage can still support high criticality.  
Calculation relevance: supports interruption loss or workaround cost.  
Common pitfalls: judging critical systems only by adoption rate.

## 9. Value Decay

Required data: opportunity window, original opportunity value or proxy, expected decay rate, delay period, competitor/substitute risk.  
Optional data: seasonal demand, policy window, customer wait tolerance, data validity period.  
Suggested owner: business owner, strategy owner, market owner.  
Why it matters: some value disappears when delivered late.  
Calculation relevance: supports remaining opportunity value or delay loss.  
Common pitfalls: inventing decay without owner confirmation.

## 10. Compliance / Regulatory Timing

Required data: external or audit deadline, risk consequence, affected scope, potential fine/shutdown/business restriction, owner confirmation.  
Optional data: remediation plan, risk grade, audit finding, legal interpretation.  
Suggested owner: legal, compliance, security, QA, audit owner.  
Why it matters: timing can reduce risk exposure or avoid non-compliance consequences.  
Calculation relevance: supports exposure-based risk view, usually not single-point ROI.  
Common pitfalls: calculating precise ROI without compliance owner input.

## 11. Risk Reduction

Required data: risk event type, potential impact, probability or risk grade, reduction mechanism, affected scope, owner endorsement.  
Optional data: historical incidents, external benchmark, audit findings, control maturity.  
Suggested owner: risk, compliance, security, QA, data governance owner.  
Why it matters: risk products protect against uncertain but material downside.  
Calculation relevance: supports expected risk reduction value or qualitative risk tier.  
Common pitfalls: judging risk tools by adoption rate only; false precision in probability.

## 12. Opportunity Enablement

Required data: enabled opportunity, pilots or scenarios supported, future pipeline, reuse potential, validation milestones.  
Optional data: time-to-start reduction, strategic initiative linkage, option value narrative.  
Suggested owner: strategy owner, innovation owner, business sponsor, product owner.  
Why it matters: enablement value creates future choices rather than current ROI.  
Calculation relevance: supports milestone, option, and pipeline hypotheses.  
Common pitfalls: counting future opportunities as current realized value.

## 13. Platform Enablement

Required data: downstream applications, teams onboarded, reuse count, avoided duplicate builds, platform run cost, dependency criticality.  
Optional data: API calls, onboarding time, incident reduction, time-to-market reduction, support load.  
Suggested owner: platform owner, architecture owner, D&T owner, finance for cost allocation.  
Why it matters: platform value is distributed across users and downstream products.  
Calculation relevance: supports avoided duplicate build, reuse, operational efficiency, and risk value.  
Common pitfalls: judging platform value only by direct single-product ROI.

## 14. Strategic Capability

Required data: strategic capability supported, milestone completion, pilots supported, reusable assets, sponsor confirmation.  
Optional data: trained users, roadmap linkage, maturity assessment, future reuse plan.  
Suggested owner: strategy sponsor, capability owner, D&T/product owner.  
Why it matters: capability value needs strategic relevance and milestone evidence.  
Calculation relevance: supports milestone-based value and future enablement narrative.  
Common pitfalls: forcing short-term ROI; weak sponsor linkage.

## 15. Learning / Validation Value

Required data: hypotheses tested, experiment cost, time box, learning result, next go/no-go criteria.  
Optional data: user feedback, pilot adoption, avoided wrong investment estimate, validated/invalidated assumptions.  
Suggested owner: innovation owner, product owner, business sponsor.  
Why it matters: learning value is only useful when it informs a decision.  
Calculation relevance: supports avoided wrong investment and next-stage confidence.  
Common pitfalls: endless pilots without hypotheses, time limit, or decision criteria.

## Directional Value Calculation Patterns

Use these patterns to generate the `Directional Value Calculation` section in the final output. These are public-facing calculation directions, not a formal Valence valuation model.

Do not use a formal aggregate value label for this section. Do not add proxy metrics such as NPS, CSAT, page views, adoption, and usage frequency directly into a monetary total. First separate value components, then identify which metrics drive money, which metrics prove adoption, and which metrics are only leading indicators.

Do not invent default attribution ratios, discount factors, industry benchmarks, or conversion assumptions. If the user has not provided them, mark them as data to confirm. Do not label self-service deflection or productivity gain as direct Cost Saving unless there is budget, vendor, outsourced service, or finance-confirmed spend reduction.

### Revenue Growth

Directional calculation:

```text
Incremental gross profit =
impacted base x conversion/order lift x average order value x gross margin x attribution ratio
```

Use traffic, leads, conversion, order count, average order value, gross margin, and attribution evidence. Treat revenue as a hypothesis when attribution is weak.

### Cost Saving

Directional calculation:

```text
Annual direct saving =
original annual cost - new annual cost
```

Use only when the cost reduction is visible in budget, contract, procurement, or finance-confirmed spend.

### Cost Avoidance

Directional calculation:

```text
Avoided cost =
avoided FTE/external days/procurement/duplicate builds x unit cost x attribution ratio
```

Use a credible counterfactual baseline. Do not present avoided future spend as confirmed saving.

### Productivity Gain

Directional calculation:

```text
Productivity value =
active users or annual transaction volume x usage frequency x labor time saved per use x labor hourly cost x discount factor
```

Use real labor time saved, not elapsed waiting time. Apply a discount factor when adoption or time-saving evidence is weak.

### Experience / Service Quality Improvement

Directional calculation:

```text
Directional experience value =
service cost reduction
+ complaint/ticket reduction value
+ retention/repeat-purchase improvement value
```

Component directions:

```text
Service cost reduction / cost avoidance signal =
self-service completions x confirmed share that would have become assisted service x assisted-service cost per case x attribution ratio to confirm
```

```text
Complaint/ticket reduction value =
reduced complaints or tickets x handling cost per case x attribution ratio
```

```text
Retention/repeat-purchase improvement value =
affected users x retention or repeat-purchase lift x annual value per user x gross margin x attribution ratio
```

NPS / CSAT are usually leading indicators. They enter value calculation only when linked to retention, repeat purchase, complaint reduction, service cost reduction, or another monetizable outcome.

### Decision Quality / Management Visibility

Directional calculation:

```text
Directional decision value =
manual reporting productivity value
+ attributed business improvement from changed decisions
```

Use decision actions and affected business metrics. Page views alone are not value evidence.

### Deadline-Driven Business Value / Value Decay

Directional calculation:

```text
Delay value =
expected value per period x delayed periods x decay or missed-window ratio
```

Use only when the deadline or value decay window is real and owner-confirmed.

### Operational Continuity

Directional calculation:

```text
Continuity value =
avoided downtime x loss per hour/day
+ avoided workaround labor cost
```

Use SLA, downtime, incident frequency, loss per period, and workaround evidence.

### Compliance / Regulatory Timing

Directional calculation:

```text
Exposure reduction =
potential impact x probability or risk tier x reduced exposure period
```

Use ranges when probability is uncertain. Require legal, compliance, security, QA, or audit owner confirmation.

### Risk Reduction

Directional calculation:

```text
Risk reduction value =
potential impact x probability x risk reduction ratio
```

Usually output as a range or risk-tier logic. Do not judge risk products by adoption rate alone.

### Platform Enablement

Directional calculation:

```text
Platform value =
avoided duplicate build
+ downstream reuse value
+ operational efficiency value
+ risk or continuity value
```

Avoid double counting downstream business value. Separate direct platform value from attributed downstream value.

### Opportunity Enablement

Directional calculation:

```text
Opportunity enablement value =
validated future pipeline or option value
+ reuse potential
+ milestone progress
```

Do not count future opportunity as current realized value. Define next validation milestone.

### Strategic Capability

Directional calculation:

```text
Strategic capability value =
capability milestone evidence
+ sponsor-confirmed strategic relevance
+ reusable assets or pilots supported
```

Do not force short-term ROI. Use milestone-based evidence and sponsor confirmation.

### Learning / Validation Value

Directional calculation:

```text
Learning value =
avoided wrong investment
+ validated learning
+ next-stage decision confidence
```

Require explicit hypothesis, time box, cost of experiment, and go/no-go criteria. Learning value must not justify endless pilots.
